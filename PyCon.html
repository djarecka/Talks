I know you are eager to get on with the hands-on session but there are some requirements that need to be satisfied. Make sure you have the following installed:

  • Text editor
  • Jupyter notebooks or JupyterLab
  • nbval and nbdime
  • pytest
  • pandas
  • Docker


Also you need to have a GitHub account (note that a GitLab account also works) and a Travis CI account (make sure to get it from travis-ci.org)

Setting yourself for success

Nobody wants to do scrummy science/research/analysis ... planning will prevent you from doing so Start with the following pillars:
  • What is your MVP?
  • Get to know your data
  • Who will use my data and my code?
  • Who and for how long is this going to be maintained?

Treat your digital assets with love

The results are important but the process you followed to get there is just as important: your scripts, null results, and iterations can make a huge difference to others
